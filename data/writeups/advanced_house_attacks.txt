The House of Water is a complex technique to gain control over tcache metadata.
An attacker creates a linked list in a small bin containing three chunks.
By overwriting the least significant byte of the fwd pointer of the first chunk and the bk pointer of the last chunk, they redirect the list to a fake chunk inside the tcache_perthread_struct.
When the tcache for that size class is emptied, a subsequent allocation from the small bin will return the fake chunk, giving the attacker control over tcache metadata.

House of Tangerine is a modernized version of House of Orange that works on glibc 2.34 and later.
An attacker uses a heap overflow to corrupt the size of the top_chunk, ensuring it remains page-aligned but is smaller than a subsequent allocation request.
When the large allocation is requested, sysmalloc is called and, seeing the top_chunk is too small and corrupted, it calls _int_free on the old top_chunk.
The old top_chunk is moved into the unsorted bin or tcache, allowing the attacker to perform further attacks like tcache poisoning without an explicit free.

The House of Spirit attack allows an attacker to allocate a chunk at an arbitrary address by forging a fake chunk header.
The attacker writes a valid size field and a sane next-chunk size at the target location.
Then, the attacker calls free() on the target address, which adds the fake chunk into a fastbin or tcache.
The next malloc for that size class will return the attacker-controlled address.
