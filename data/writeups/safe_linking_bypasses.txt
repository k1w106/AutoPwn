Decrypt Safe Linking is a technique to recover original pointers from the masked pointers introduced in glibc 2.32.
Safe linking XORs pointers in tcache and fastbins with (chunk_address >> 12).
An attacker can recover the mask by observing that the first 12 bits of the pointer are known or can be leaked.
By iteratively XORing and shifting, the full original pointer can be reconstructed from the masked value.

Safe Link Double Protect is a method to bypass safe linking using tcache metadata control.
If an attacker can control tcache metadata, they can point a tcache bin entry to another tcache bin entry that already contains a masked pointer.
When this second entry is allocated and then linked into a new tcache, the safe linking mechanism XORs it again with the same key.
Since (ptr ^ key) ^ key equals ptr, the protection is effectively removed, and the original pointer is restored in the new tcache bin.
